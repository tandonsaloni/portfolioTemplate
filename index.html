About

Collapsible Group 1

I am hard-working and results-oriented with over nine years of experience in leading and managing research and consultancy projects (in the social and human development space) focused on achieving sustainable, equitable outcomes for vulnerable and marginalized communities. These projects typically focused on providing technical advisory services to design or refine policies, processes launched by the government and products by institutions catering to these segments. My key skill sets include – ability to conduct qualitative (using ATLAS.ti, Excel -manual coding) and quantitative research for policy analysis (using R, Python, Excel), as well as monitoring and evaluation of programs, interventions, and policies.

Through my work, I have been able to gain extensive knowledge across domains of financial inclusion, social protection, women’s economic empowerment, digital identity, climate change, renewable energy, and public health. Since 2016 my primary area of work has been to develop solutions (both policy level and operational) that help improve the level of financial inclusion and social protection amongst the most vulnerable (especially women, migrants, and gig workers) in India, and more recently Kenya. I have also worked on projects that support increasing women’s access and usage of public health systems, and on assessing the quality of education offered at the last mile in India. My current research at the University of Pennsylvania focuses on exploring the intersection between gender and energy, as well as gender and health. I am also working on identifying barriers that restrict women from accessing healthcare services in cultural contexts such as that of India.

I am extremely comfortable working with a varied range of situations and with culturally and technically diverse stakeholders. Moving forward, I want to develop technology and data-backed solutions to modify detrimental social norms and improve gender-based outcomes at scale.
